> cat ‘Study Spaces’

Some of my favorite Boston-area cafes to study in.

Perfect for a warm cup of coffee and a soft ambience to get cozy in the terminal for a few hours.

Pavement Coffee

My go-to spot for a productive morning, nestled conveniently close to campus. Sweet matcha lattes, hearty bagels. Spacious but quickly fills with the gentle hum of studious, socializing college kids.

Ogawa Coffee

Coming from Japan with their first international shop here, Ogawa undoubtedly serves the best matcha in Boston. Specifically notable is their rose matcha latte where the aroma of sprinkled petals accompanies every balanced sip. Expansive windows that let sunlight illuminate the space, and creative elevated seating. With its address on Milk Street, Ogawa is clearly the perfect study cafe.

Vester Cafe

Sleek and contemporary cafe inspired by a Copenhagen neighborhood. Food, drink, and interior are simple, but done well. Personal favorite is their matcha latte with a coconut ube cloud.

Maruichi Select

Tucked away in a grocery store with floor seating in a separate, shoes-off room. They serve foamy lattes that are deep in flavor (using Ogawa matcha), and offer a tasty selection of Japanese dishes.

Curio Coffee

Small, intimate cafe with a steady stream of regulars getting their usual and catching up with the baristas. Known for their Liège waffles studded with pearl sugar. Laptop-free on the weekends.

Gypsy Place Coffee

Vibrant throws and pillows adorn this cafe’s eclectic bohemian style. Whimsical and friendly.

Faro Cafe

Charming and simple, with warm lighting and thriving plants. Sometimes hosts live music, and pop-up art exhibitions. Always laptop-free, so best for reading papers or studying with friends.
