Cybersecurity Analyst at Draper

12-19-2023

Just finished my first co-op experience: 7 months at Draper Labs as a Cybersecurity Analyst working in the SOC.

Cybersecurity can be a lot of fun, especially when presented in a gamified way like in CTFs or competitions. However, I realized the implementation of cybersecurity in a corporate setting is very different, often because of financial, administrative, or compliance restraints that make meaningful progress difficult. At least in my role, it was mostly about maintenance rather than improvements. While triaging daily alerts from Splunk, Carbon Black, and Proofpoint improved my skills in threat analysis, vulnerability management, and incident response, I didn’t find it satisfying. Hours spent on false positives, and there was no tangible result of my work. No sense of progress.

I realized how much I missed programming and building real solutions, and thankfully my manager understood and gave me several coding projects. I did a lot of work with Tenable’s API to automate vulnerability scanning and parsing results, and built a database of network enclave information with a corresponding user-friendly front-end. I enjoyed this work much more. It felt creative and purposeful, as I was actively making security operations more efficient and resilient. Before my co-op, all domains of cybersecurity seemed interesting, but now I was able to narrow my interests.

Figuring out what I like and want took significant self-reflection about what makes me happy- in a job and in life. I think I feel most fulfilled by three core areas: 1) my learning and personal growth, 2) my impact on the world, and 3) my relationships. For future positions, I’d prioritize a role where I’m pushed to develop and expand my skillset, where my work has a visible positive impact, and where the work culture is bubbling, social, and collaborative to encourage meaningful connections.

I am so grateful towards the CISO, my manager, and the team for all their guidance and support in this experience. In my last conversation with the CISO, I thanked him, told him I hope we cross paths again, and teased that maybe it’ll be when I’m a CISO too. There’s a long way to go…
